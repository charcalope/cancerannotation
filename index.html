Help Annotate Cancer Research

The Goal

You'll be shown a series of summarized article texts, like the one below.

The drug was effective at treating the cancer, and is recommended for further study.

Label: effective

Each text has a true label of effective or noneffective and your task is to give your best guess as to which it is.

What contributes to the label?

Here is some of the language of the effective class:

  • Inhibiting the cancer
  • Contributing to a positive outcome
  • Causing a decrease in negative outcomes
  • Being active against the cancer

    • Here is some of the language of the noneffective class:

  • Had no effect on the cancer or outcomes
  • Caused or contributed to negative outcomes
  • Results were positive only for some tumors, and not others

    • Some Examples

    The treatment should not be chosen as standard treatment in patients with the cancer. However, the treatment represents a useful alternative in patients who prefer oral treatment or when socioeconomic considerations are an important issue.

    Label: noneffective

    Thus, near life-long treatment with the drug in rats resulted in dose and time dependent formation of the cancer.

    Label: noneffective

    The results suggested that the drug might be a promising target for clinical therapy for the cancer.

    Label: effective
